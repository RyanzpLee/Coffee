Equipment

Down below are 6 different types of coffee brewing equipment that you may not have heard of, but are widely used by people enthusaistic about coffee. You can click on the cards to learn more about the specific equipment.

Each different piece of equipment has different advantages and disadvantages and and bring out different flavours of your coffee beans that you may not otherwise have tasted. Some brewing methods use paper filters, whilst others do not.

Hario V60

The Hario V60 is a simple drip coffee maker that is popular amongst many coffee enthusiasts and within 3 minutes, you can brew an incredibly tasty coffee

Kalita Wave

The Kalita Wave is a dripper style brewer but compared to the Hario V60, it sports a flat bed with three small dripper holes to give you a more even extraction and controlled flow-rate

AeroPress

The Aeropress uses an immersion brew method. It uses finely ground coffee and has a relatively short brew time. You push down on a plunger creating pressure to improve extracting and flavour. It is particular mobile and well suited for travel.

Chemex

This distinct hourglass-shaped flask utilises particularly thick paper filters which produces a much cleaner cup of coffee as less oils from the coffee is allowed through.

Espresso Machine

This machine forces pressurized water through a puck of coffee and results in a thick concentrated coffee.

French Press

The french press is a very popular immerision brewing method and results in a full bodied cup of coffee. It is extremely simple to use and does not require any paper filters.
